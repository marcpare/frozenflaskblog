Process and Approach

Why are so many web user interfaces over-complicated and ineffective? And why do they still cost so much to build?

Modern web browsers have enabled sophisticated user interfaces. Unfortunately, traditional approaches to UI design, such as static mockups in Photoshop, fail to capture the essential parts of modern interface design: interaction, animation, and responsiveness to screen-size.

Cost escalates because of the interaction of graphic designers and software engineers. Graphic designers without software background fail to consider fundamental implementation problems. Software engineers are trained to move slowly and carefully, rather than quickly through the space of product possibilities. Passing a UI design problem between these two competing sides blows up costs.

What can be done?

Product prototypes in HTML and Javascript accurately capture the end user experience. Piping in real application data reveals its contours and character, avoiding surprises down the line when the Photoshop mockups turns out to be impossible to implement.

It is much easier to plan and manage the development of a product that has been prototyped at high fidelity. This, of course, greatly reduces cost and mitigates execution risk.
